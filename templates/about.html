SOS: Suspicious Oil Ships Tracker



"Fossil fuel exports are a key enabler of Russia’s military buildup and brutal aggression against Ukraine."


Putin’s War against Ukraine is heavily dependent on sales of fossil exports, including sea transfers of oil. In the first 100 days, Russia eanred 93 billion euros in revenue.

As Russian oil is increasingly shipped to more distant markets, more tanker capacity than ever before is needed. This is a key vulnerability — strong sanctions against tankers transporting Russian crude would significantly limit the scope for this kind of rerouting of Russia’s exports. In April-May, 68% of deliveries of Russian crude oil were made with ships owned by EU, UK and Norwegian companies, with Greek tankers alone carrying 43%. For deliveries to India and the Middle East, the share was even higher at 80%. 97% of the tankers were insured in just three countries, UK, Norway and Sweden.

Something Strange is going on in the Aegean Sea.

Lately, many ships are conducting STS (Ship to Ship Transfers) which are questionable. Are they transferring Russian oil and skirting the sanctions placed on Russia to curtail their financing of the invasion of Ukraine? SOS tracker is a platform that monitors these transfers in order to bring awareness of whether other countries are violating the sanctions. We want to know if everyone is all in on the sanctions and willing to help end Putin’s war.
